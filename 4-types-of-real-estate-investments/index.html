4 Types Of Real Estate Investments

  • Property, Real Estate, Real Estate Advice
  • March 20, 2019

Real estate is one of the oldest, safest and most popular asset classes however most new investors in real estate have not been enlightened on the types of real estate investments in existence.

If you want to make a great deal in real estate investment, you will have to consider the type of property that you have in mind to invest in because all types of real estate investment has its benefits and pitfalls, which includes unique cash flow cycles, leasing traditions, and standards of what is considered appropriate.

Therefore you’ll want to study them well before you start adding them to your property wish list. The four major types of real estate that you can invest in include the following:

1. Residential properties

They are the most common basic, demanding and essential real estate properties because they comprise the highest number of available properties. They include but are not limited to: Bungalows, apartments, and houses. The most common is single-family homes. There are also condominiums, duplexes, three storey, high-value homes, and vacation homes.

If you want to find a great residential property in any given area, find a local agent who is more knowledgeable and would be in the best position to tell you the best residential properties and price in the local area.

2. Commercial Properties

As the name implies these includes but is not limited to shopping centers, strip malls, medical and educational buildings, hotels and offices, single buildings with one large retail outlet and roadside booths and kiosks.

commercial properties are always in demand in real estate properties. More so they usually provide a stable income to the property owners per annum. Some of the factors identified to determine the demand and value of commercial property are as follows; visibility of the property, people traffic, The socio-economic status and income levels of the people who frequently use the commercial outlet.

3. Industrial properties

From the word industrial, these include manufacturing buildings and properties. The buildings can be used for either for research, production, as a store/warehouse and distribution of goods.

These properties generally have less operating costs than other properties. They also have lower management and maintenance costs and vary depending what it’s being used for. Also, these buildings usually have to be configured and redesigned according to their usage. For example, high ceilings may be necessary if you’re in search of a production building/ storehouse.

4. Landed properties

These Includes bare/vacant lands, working farms, and ranches, thereby the owner would either build on it, figure out the kind of structure he/she wants to emerge from the land or leave the bare land as it is i.e probably use it as a means of sustenance or income through farming.

The subcategories within vacant lands include undeveloped, early development or reuse and site assembly.

having gone through this article you are being advised to choose your real estate investment wisely and remember that each type of property comes with its advantages and disadvantages that will determine your cash flow.
